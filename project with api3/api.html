Brain CT scan

A brain CT scan (Computed Tomography) is a medical imaging test that uses X-rays and computer technology to create detailed and precise images of the brain. It is commonly performed to detect brain injuries, bleeding, tumors, or strokes. During the procedure, the patient lies on a table that slides into a circular machine which rotates around the head to capture images from different angles. Sometimes, a contrast dye is injected to highlight blood vessels or specific structures. The scan usually takes about 5â€“10 minutes and is painless. Afterward, patients can resume normal activities immediately. It provides fast and accurate results, though it involves a small amount of radiation. It is considered one of the most essential emergency tests for evaluating critical brain conditions.

What is the importance of a CT scan?

A CT scan is important because it allows doctors to quickly identify serious brain problems such as bleeding, trauma, or tumors, helping them make immediate and life-saving decisions.
